Theoretical framework
Baseline model — inspired by Laber et al. (2023):
x(t+1)=A⋅x(t)x(t+1) = A \cdot x(t)x(t+1)=A⋅x(t)
Fixed technical coefficients, no absorption, worst-case propagation.
Your extension — dynamic operator:
x(t+1)=M⋅x(t)+fx(t+1) = M \cdot x(t) + fx(t+1)=M⋅x(t)+f
M=(I−αD)AM = (I - \alpha D) AM=(I−αD)A
Where:

α = substitution efficiency = 0.30 (from spectral stability sweep)
D = diagonal matrix of damping coefficients d_j
f = final demand vector (held constant)

Your novel contribution — institutionally augmented damping:
dj=(1−Hj)⋅ϕc(j)d_j = (1 - H_j) \cdot \phi_{c(j)}dj​=(1−Hj​)⋅ϕc(j)​
Where:

H_j = Herfindahl concentration on normalized input shares (input_dist column j)
φ_c(j) = normalized Logistics Performance Index (LPI) score of country c(j), scaled to [0,1]
No TAU needed — normalization handles near-zero links naturally

Economic interpretation:

H_j measures structural diversification of sector j's input sourcing
φ_c captures institutional capacity to actually exercise that diversification
A sector in a low-institution country is more fragile even if nominally diversified
Two sectors with identical H_j but different φ_c will have different shock absorption
